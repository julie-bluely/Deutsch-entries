About the diary

Learning to belong, one word at a time

„Eine neue Sprache zu lernen heißt, ein zweites Mal Kind zu sein — nur höflicher.“

Hallo, ich bin die Person hinter Deutsch Entries.

This blog is a record of a small, stubborn project: learning German not as a task to be finished, but as a place to live in. I started with a phrasebook, a borrowed grammar, and a fierce love of three things that, for me, sit at the heart of European culture — the patient geometry of Persian rugs, the impossible stone of cathedrals, and the bright, defiant cheerfulness of a lemon in winter.

Each entry here is written in German, on purpose. At the beginning, the posts were four sentences long and full of mistakes. They still have mistakes — but now they are paragraphs, sometimes pages. You are watching a language arrive in real time. The interface stays in English so visitors can follow along; the writing stays in German so I keep growing.

Why German, of all things?

Because it has words for feelings I never had names for. Because the grammar is a puzzle that rewards patience, like weaving. And because somewhere along the way, between the umlauts and the long compound nouns, it stopped feeling foreign and started feeling like a second home I was slowly building, brick by careful brick.
